## NOTES:
- Organizational requirements exists to help protect sensitive data, reduce legal and regulatory risks ensure staff behave, prevent data misuse, safeguard IP, and maintain trust with clients
- IP = Intellectual property - creation of the mind. E.g., designs, software, written content, brand names and inventions. 
- IP gives the owner the ability to use, share, sell, license and control
- Types of IP 
    1. Copyright: Protect original works and giv es creators exclusive rights to use their work. This applies to software code, website content, photos, training materials
    2. Patents: Protect inventions, designs, processes. Gives the inventor the license to make/sell the invention. This applies to hardware designs, algorithms, unique software features. 
    3. Trademarks: Protect brand identifiers like names, logo, symbols and slogans. This applies to companies like Apple, Google, Microsoft, etc... 

- Common ethical principles that exist in the workplace are:
    - Honesty/Integrity
    - Confidentiality
    - Transparency 
    - Professional conduct
    - Responsible use of tech 

- ICT Professionals must follow:
    - Privacy Laws
    - Workplace policies
    - Copyright legislation
    - IP ownership rules
    - Cybersecurity requirements
    - Duty of care
